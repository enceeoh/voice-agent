# Fuseworks Electrical — Quoting Rules and Scope (FW-QR-2026-03)

## 1. Estimates vs Fixed Prices

- All prices quoted from a phone call or voicemail are ESTIMATES, subject
  to a site survey. Every written estimate must state this.
- Fixed prices are only issued after a survey.
- Estimates are valid for 30 days.

## 2. Older Properties — the Consumer Unit Rule

- If the consumer unit (fuse box) predates 2000, or its age is unknown,
  an EICR inspection is REQUIRED before any EV charger or solar
  installation can proceed, and a consumer unit replacement is likely.
- Quotes for EV/solar work on such properties MUST include the EICR as a
  line item and flag the consumer unit replacement as a probable
  additional cost. Do not present the headline install price alone.

## 3. Access and Location Surcharges

- Congestion Charge zone or ULEZ-adjacent central London jobs: add £45
  per day on site (covers charge and parking).
- Terraced houses with no off-street parking: note that EV charger
  feasibility depends on a driveway or legal cable-run arrangement;
  cross-pavement charging requires council permission — flag this on the
  estimate rather than assuming.
- No scaffolding on the customer's own arrangement: we only install
  solar from scaffolding we erect and certify.

## 4. Lead Times and Deposits

- EV charger installations: typically 2 weeks from survey.
- Solar installations: typically 6–8 weeks from survey (panel supply and
  DNO approval).
- Jobs over £2,000 require a 25% deposit on booking.
- Jobs over £10,000 require director sign-off before the estimate is sent.

## 5. Scope of Work

We do: all domestic electrical work, EV chargers, solar PV and battery
storage, EICRs, consumer units, rewires, fault-finding.

We do NOT do: gas or heating work (boilers, hobs), plumbing of any kind,
roofing repairs (we mount solar on sound roofs but do not repair roofs),
appliance repair, TV/aerial installation, or commercial three-phase
industrial work. For out-of-scope requests, decline politely and
recommend the customer finds the relevant trade; do not name specific
competitor firms and never attempt to price out-of-scope work.

Grey areas: where a fault MAY lie in the electrical supply rather than
the appliance itself (e.g. a hot tub, oven, or machine that hums, trips
the circuit, or loses power), offer a fault-finding visit at the standard
labour rate to diagnose the supply side — while making clear that we do
not repair the appliance itself, and if the fault turns out to be inside
the appliance, the customer will need the relevant specialist.

## 6. Estimate Contents

Every estimate must include: itemised prices from the price book, the
estimate-pending-survey disclaimer, applicable surcharges, lead time,
deposit terms if applicable, and a list of anything the customer needs
to confirm (missing information from their enquiry).
